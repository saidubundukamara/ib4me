## ARTICLE 5: VESTING SCHEDULE

### 5.1 Vesting Terms

All equity allocated to Founders shall be subject to a vesting schedule as follows:

- **Total Vesting Period:** 24 months (2 years)
- **Cliff Period:** 6 months
- **Vesting Frequency:** Quarterly (after cliff)

### 5.2 Vesting Mechanics

a) **Cliff:** No equity shall vest until the Founder has completed 6 months of active participation. Upon completion of the cliff period, 25% of the Founder's equity shall vest.

b) **Post-Cliff Vesting:** After the cliff period, the remaining 75% of equity shall vest quarterly in equal installments over the remaining 18 months (approximately 12.5% per quarter).

c) **Vesting Commencement Date:** The vesting schedule commences on the Effective Date of this Agreement.

### 5.3 Acceleration Events

Full acceleration of unvested equity shall occur upon:

a) Acquisition of the Company by a third party
b) Initial Public Offering (IPO)
c) Death or permanent disability of a Founder

### 5.4 Treatment of Unvested Equity Upon Departure

If a Founder ceases to be an active participant before full vesting:

a) **Vested equity** remains with the departing Founder
b) **Unvested equity** shall be forfeited and redistributed among remaining Founders proportionally, or held by the Company

## ARTICLE 7: DECISION-MAKING

### 7.1 Routine Decisions

Day-to-day decisions within a Founder's area of responsibility may be made independently by that Founder, provided they:
- Are within approved budget limits
- Do not materially affect other departments
- Are consistent with Company policies

### 7.2 Major Decisions Requiring Majority Vote

The following decisions require approval by Founders holding more than 50% of equity:
- Hiring of employees or contractors
- Expenditures exceeding SLE _____________ [AMOUNT]
- Marketing campaigns and partnerships
- Operational policy changes
- Entering into contracts valued under SLE _____________ [AMOUNT]

### 7.3 Fundamental Decisions Requiring Supermajority (75%)

The following decisions require approval by Founders holding at least 75% of equity:
- Raising external capital or taking on debt
- Acquiring or merging with another company
- Selling significant Company assets
- Entering new markets or jurisdictions
- Changes to the Company's core business model
- Expenditures or contracts exceeding SLE _____________ [AMOUNT]
- Adding new Founders or issuing new equity

### 7.4 Unanimous Decisions

The following decisions require unanimous approval of all Founders:
- Sale of the Company
- Dissolution of the Company
- Amendment of this Agreement
- Removal of a Founder
- Changes to equity allocation (except as provided in vesting and forfeiture sections)

### 7.5 Voting Procedure

a) Voting may be conducted at meetings or via written consent (including electronic communication).

b) For formal decisions, votes shall be documented in writing and maintained in Company records.

c) Each Founder's vote shall be weighted according to their equity percentage.

### 7.6 Deadlock Resolution

In case of a deadlock on major decisions:
1. Founders shall discuss the matter and attempt to reach consensus
2. If consensus cannot be reached within 7 days, an external mediator may be engaged
3. The mediator's recommendation shall be advisory, with final decision by vote

## ARTICLE 8: NON-PERFORMANCE AND REMEDIATION

### 8.1 Performance Expectations

Each Founder is expected to:
- Fulfill their role responsibilities as outlined in Article 6
- Meet their minimum time commitment
- Participate actively in meetings and decision-making
- Communicate proactively about challenges or delays
- Act in the best interests of the Company

### 8.2 Definition of Non-Performance

Non-performance includes but is not limited to:
- Consistent failure to meet time commitment requirements
- Failure to complete assigned tasks without reasonable justification
- Lack of responsiveness to communications for more than 7 consecutive days without prior notice
- Failure to attend 3 or more consecutive scheduled meetings without valid excuse
- Conduct detrimental to the Company's reputation or operations
- Breach of confidentiality or fiduciary duties

### 8.3 Remediation Process

**Step 1: Informal Discussion (Week 1-2)**
- Other Founders shall privately address concerns with the underperforming Founder
- The Founder shall have opportunity to explain circumstances and propose solutions

**Step 2: Formal Written Warning (Week 3-4)**
- If issues persist, a formal written warning shall be issued
- The warning shall specify:
  - Nature of the performance issues
  - Specific expectations for improvement
  - Timeline for improvement (typically 30 days)
  - Consequences of continued non-performance

**Step 3: Mediation (Week 5-6)**
- If improvement is not achieved, mandatory mediation shall be conducted
- The mediator shall be a neutral third party agreed upon by all Founders
- The mediation shall result in a documented action plan or determination

**Step 4: Buyback and Separation (If Mediation Fails)**
- If mediation does not resolve the issue, the Company may exercise its buyback right
- The non-performing Founder's unvested equity shall be forfeited
- Vested equity may be purchased by the Company or remaining Founders at Fair Market Value, with a discount of up to 25% to account for the circumstances
- The departing Founder shall retain no operational role or decision-making authority

### 8.4 Good Leaver vs. Bad Leaver

**Good Leaver:** A Founder who departs due to death, disability, or circumstances beyond their control shall retain all vested equity without discount.

**Bad Leaver:** A Founder who departs due to:
- Voluntary resignation without good reason
- Termination for cause (non-performance, breach of duties, misconduct)
Shall be subject to equity buyback at a 25% discount to Fair Market Value for vested equity, and forfeiture of all unvested equity.

## ARTICLE 11: NON-COMPETE AND NON-SOLICITATION

### 11.1 Non-Compete

During the term of this Agreement and for **1 year** following departure, no Founder shall:
- Directly or indirectly engage in a business that competes with the Company's core crowdfunding platform services in Sierra Leone
- Assist others in competing with the Company

### 11.2 Non-Solicitation

For **2 years** following departure, no Founder shall:
- Solicit or attempt to hire Company employees or contractors
- Solicit or divert Company users, donors, or partners

## ARTICLE 12: DEPARTURE AND EXIT

### 12.1 Voluntary Departure

A Founder wishing to leave the Company voluntarily shall:
- Provide a minimum of 60 days written notice
- Complete orderly transition of responsibilities
- Return all Company property and materials
- Execute any necessary documents for transfer of duties

### 12.2 Right of First Refusal

If a Founder wishes to sell or transfer their equity, the Company and other Founders shall have the right of first refusal to purchase such equity at Fair Market Value or the offered price, whichever is lower.

### 12.3 Drag-Along Rights

If Founders holding 75% or more of equity agree to sell the Company, all Founders must participate in the sale on the same terms.

### 12.4 Tag-Along Rights

If any Founder receives an offer to sell their equity, other Founders may participate in the sale on the same terms, proportionally to their holdings.
