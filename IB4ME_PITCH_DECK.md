## Slide 3: Our Solution
**[TIMING: 45 seconds]**

**[ON SLIDE]**

**IB4ME** — *"In Being For Me"*
Africa's First Medical-Verified Crowdfunding Platform

**How It Works:**
1. **Create** — Patient or family creates a campaign
2. **Verify** — Hospital partner confirms the medical need
3. **Share** — Campaign goes live on WhatsApp
4. **Receive** — Funds delivered via mobile money

**3 Key Differentiators:**
- Hospital verification (trust)
- Local payments (Orange Money, AfriMoney)
- WhatsApp-first (how Sierra Leoneans communicate)

---

**[SAY THIS]**

"IB4ME — 'In Being For Me' — is Africa's first medical-verified crowdfunding platform.

Here's how it works. A patient or their family creates a campaign with their medical details and documentation. Our hospital partners — starting with Connaught Hospital — verify that the medical need is real. Once verified, the campaign goes live and gets shared on WhatsApp — which is how Sierra Leoneans actually communicate. Donors can contribute using Orange Money, AfriMoney, or credit cards for the diaspora. And funds go directly to the patient via mobile money.

Three things make us different. First, *hospital verification* — every campaign is medically verified, which builds trust and prevents fraud. Second, *local payments* — we support the mobile money systems people actually use. Third, *WhatsApp-first* — campaigns spread where conversations already happen.

We're not adapting a Western platform to Africa. We built this *for* Sierra Leone."

"Why will we win?

First, pricing. We charge just 1 percent — compared to GoFundMe's 2.9 percent plus transaction fees, or Ketto's 5 percent. When you're raising money for a sick family member, every dollar matters.

Second, trust. We're the *only* platform with hospital-based medical verification. Every other crowdfunding platform relies on self-reported information. Third, accessibility. GoFundMe only accepts credit cards. In Sierra Leone, less than 5 percent of people have credit cards. We support Orange Money and AfriMoney — the payment methods 30 percent of adults already use, and growing 20 percent annually.

We didn't take an American platform and try to make it work in Africa. We built this from the ground up for Sierra Leone."

- **1%** — Our platform fee (vs. 3-5% competitors)

### Anticipated Questions

**Q: Why Sierra Leone specifically?**
A: Our founding team is from Sierra Leone. We understand the culture, payment systems, and healthcare challenges intimately. This is our home — and we're building for our community first before expanding regionally.

**Q: How do you prevent fraud?**
A: Hospital verification. Every campaign is reviewed and verified by our medical partners before going live. This is fundamentally different from self-reported platforms.

**Q: What's your path to profitability?**
A: At scale, 1% of donations plus premium hospital services. Break-even at ~8,000 successful campaigns annually. But the immediate focus is proving the model and achieving growth-round metrics.

**Q: Why not just use GoFundMe?**
A: GoFundMe doesn't support mobile money payments — which is how 95%+ of Sierra Leoneans transact. They also don't have medical verification, and their fees are 3x higher than ours.

**Q: What happens if you don't raise the full $35K?**
A: We can launch with less, but with reduced marketing reach. The platform is built — we need capital for operations and customer acquisition, not development.
